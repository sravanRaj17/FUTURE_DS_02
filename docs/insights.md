\# Customer Retention \& Churn Insights



\- Month-to-month contract customers show the highest churn rate compared to one-year and two-year contracts.

\- A significant portion of customer churn occurs within the first 6 months of subscription, indicating early-stage dissatisfaction.

\- Customers with higher monthly charges tend to have a higher probability of churn.

\- Long-tenure customers are more stable and demonstrate better retention over time.

\- Payment method plays a key role in churn, with electronic check users showing higher churn than auto-payment users.

\- Customers subscribed to multiple services generally show lower churn compared to single-service users.
